## ✅ RESOLVED: the Platinum-Expansion forestry-tools flag clears — these are real FS25 features

**Previous flag:** 4 of the manual's Forestry Machines & Tools (Hydraulic Breaker, Tree Marking
Spray, Yarder, Winches) were flagged because their matching FS25-Academy articles all stated they
required "the Platinum Expansion for Farming Simulator 22."

**Resolution:** FS25HF p.17 — the newer, fuller, confirmed-FS22-clean manual edition (see
`index.md`: FS25HF's own text explicitly introduces the Highlands Fishing expansion by name and
uses only real FS25 maps) — **still presents all four tools identically to the base manual, with
no expansion caveat.** Since FS25HF is authoritative and FS22-clean, and it consistently (across
both editions) treats these four tools as ordinary FS25 features, **the flag clears: these are
real base-FS25 content.** The Academy's "Platinum Expansion for Farming Simulator 22" claim about
these specific four tools was itself the stale/wrong information — consistent with the other 4
confirmed instances of unrevised FS22 content on that site (see `index.md`'s punch list).

**Still open, lower stakes:** "Silverrun Forest" (named in both manual editions, in the Yarder
section) doesn't match any of FS25's four now-known maps (Riverbend Springs, Hutan Pantai,
Zielonka, Kinlaig). Since the manual itself is confirmed authentic, this is most likely a named
forestry sub-location within one of those maps rather than a sign of bad content — but that's not
confirmed either. Kept on the punch list as a minor open item, not a trust concern.

## Basics (FS25HF p.16, base p.16 agrees)

- Selling wood is described as "a profitable business."
- Trees are cut with either a chainsaw or a tree harvester, depending on tree type and intent.
- A full forestry operation needs expensive machines — leasing them, or trying forestry via
  contracts first, is suggested as a lower-commitment alternative.

## Tree Types (FS25HF p.16, base p.16 agrees)

Two categories: **Conifers** (small branches, long straight trunks, "ideal for timber
production") and **Deciduous** (more crooked, thick branches, "mainly used for wood chip
production"). Decorative trees (build-menu only) aren't harvestable/profitable.

- **Deciduous trees are all cut by hand with a chainsaw.**
- **Conifers can use special wood harvesters, except cypress and stone pine**, which are
  chainsaw-only like deciduous trees.

**Academy-sourced, NOT in either FS25 manual — unverified.** A full 12-species breakdown (4
conifer: Spruce, Pine, Stone Pine, Cypress; 8 deciduous: Birch, Oak, Willow, Maple, Pagoda
Dogwood, Shagbark Hickory, American Elm, Downy Serviceberry) and a **2–24 in-game-day growth-time
range** were previously sourced to Academy news_id=367. Searched both PDFs directly for these
species names and the day-range — confirmed absent from both. Kept as a plausible-but-unverified
note; verify in-game before relying on either the species list or the growth-time figure.

## Planting & Harvesting (FS25HF p.16, base p.16 agrees)

- Trees are planted with dedicated tree planters, using sapling pallets — **unlike crop seeds,
  you must choose which specific tree type/species to buy** (not a generic "seed" purchase).
- Before selling, logs should be de-branched and cut.

**Academy-sourced, NOT in either FS25 manual — unverified.** "No field preparation needed at all
for tree planting" (no cultivating/plowing/liming/fertilizing) and the financial rationale for
deciduous→wood-chips vs. conifer→timber (uneven branching lowers raw log value) were previously
sourced to Academy news_id=376, 377, 409. Not corroborated by either PDF — plausible given the
manual's own Tree Types framing, but not confirmed.

## Transporting & Selling (FS25HF p.16, base p.16 agrees)

- Logs sell to the sawmill or go further into a factory chain (see `production.md`).
- **Price depends on log length AND straightness** of the delivered piece — "small, bent pieces
  will generally sell for less."
- A log loader is needed to transport logs.
- Bent/small pieces can instead be chipped into wood chips (via a wood chipper) and sold to the
  sawmill; wood chips need a trailer to transport.

**Academy-sourced, NOT in either FS25 manual — unverified.** Specific sale-point routing (wood
chips to Farmer's Market/Biogas Plant, logs to Carpentry/Sawmill/Biogas Plant), the cheap-vs-
expensive wood chipper equipment tradeoff, and the stump-mulching step after felling were
previously sourced to Academy news_id=378, 379, 414. Not corroborated by either PDF.

## Forestry Machines & Tools (FS25HF p.17, base p.17 agrees) — confirmed real FS25 content

- **Hydraulic Breaker:** crushes rocks embedded in forest ground; collect and sell the debris at
  the debris crusher. (Cross-reference: `fields.md` covers the equivalent field-stone tooling.)
- **Tree marking spray:** marks trees (different colors/icons available) to indicate fell-or-
  preserve status. The manual explicitly warns: "Be careful to cut down only the trees you are
  supposed to!"
- **Yarder:** connects two points (attached to a tractor at one end, "a massive tree" at the
  other) in Silverrun Forest specifically — a carriage between the two points moves logs over
  long/impassable terrain.
- **Winches:** drag logs via a tear-resistant wire rope attached to a tractor — "especially
  helpful on steep terrain."

**Academy-sourced, NOT in either FS25 manual — unverified.** Specific yarder variant numbers
(K-300-T: uphill-only, 500m span; K307c-H: bidirectional, 840m span) and winch capacity ("up to
10 logs" per implement) were previously sourced to Academy news_id=433, 434 — the same two
articles that carried the (now-cleared, see above) Platinum Expansion caveat. Since the caveat on
these articles was wrong, their other content isn't automatically trustworthy either — not
corroborated by either PDF, kept as unverified rather than promoted to fact.
